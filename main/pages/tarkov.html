PC | 2017

Escape From Tarkov is an extraction-based first person shooter game with an emphasis on realism and has PvP as well as PvE. Tarkov was created by Battlestate Games with development starting in 2012 and a beta version was released in 2017.

A unique aspect of Tarkov is it's weaponry. The level of customizability is rarely, if ever, seen in other first person shooter games. There are hundreds of weapon attachments including optics, barrel attachments, foregrips and more to customize your gun with. There's also a variety of ammo types of each caliber with each having their own damage and armor penetration characteristics.

Tarkov's gameplay is centered around the idea of extraction. In a raid, you loot items and complete quests around the map and then survive by leaving at an extraction point. If another player or NPC kills you during a raid, you lose all your gear and items you had on you, which makes the gameplay punishing and intense. These gameplay features have been key to Tarkov's success and has lead other game studios to incorporate them in their own games.
