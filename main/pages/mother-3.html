MOTHER 3

GBA | 2006

MOTHER 3 focuses on exploring the game world from a top-down perspective and engaging in turn-based combat with enemies.

Despite never being released outside of Japan, it was a critical and commercial success, and has generated a cult following.

The Impact of MOTHER 3

What was once an obscure part of Nintendo history has become one of the most enduring legacies the company has ever produced in video game history.


It received praise for its character development, stylized graphics, music, and story. These passionate fans soon began making their own MOTHER-inspired games.

The biggest of these indie RPGs is definitely UNDERTALE and to a certain extent its spiritual successor DELTARUNE.

 

UNDERTALE creator Toby Fox created a number of his own MOTHER ROM hacks before deciding to make UNDERTALE.

Another major indie game that took inspiration from MOTHER 3 is OMORI, which shares MOTHER 3's suburban adventure with an underlying tone of despair.

OMORI creator OMOCAT credits MOTHER 3 as inspiration for the zany atmosphere, stylization, and subsersive turn-based combat both games share.

Lastly, MOTHER 3 is well known for subverting the typical RPG game formula by using similar elements to curate wholesome, introspective, and non-combative gaming experiences.

 

These traits are best exemplified in games such as CELESTE and Night in the Woods.
